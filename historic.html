Historic Representations of Medusa

Medusa has been represented in various ways throughout history. Some of the more popular forms of this representations have been through the medium of art and literary forms.

Throughout ancient Greece, there were numerous references to Medusa and her curse made by ancient Greek writers including, Hyginus, Hesiod, Aeschylus, Dionysios, Skytobrachion, Herodotus. In addition references were made by Roman authors like Ovid and Pindar.



When Medusa was represented in Art, usually only her head was shown, as it contianed the most illustrative features that people were taken aback by. In many of these head representations, she is depicted as having a broad face, sometimes with tusks, and the characteristic feature of having snakes in place of hair. In other less common depictions, she is shown as having fangs, a forked tongue, and bulging eyes.
